Analysis: Tabs/Pills vs. Separate Pages

When are Tabs/Pills a better choice?

Tabs and Pills are ideal for organizing different facets of the same item or closely related information on a single screen. This approach enhances user experience by preventing page reloads and keeping the context intact. Use them when:

  • Content is closely related: The information in different tabs belongs to the same context (e.g., a user profile with tabs for "Details," "Orders," and "Settings").
  • Comparing information: Users need to frequently switch between sections to compare information.
  • Reducing clutter: You need to present a large amount of information in a structured way without overwhelming the user on initial page load.
When are separate pages better?

Separate pages are better suited for distinct, substantial topics that can stand on their own and benefit from a unique URL.

  • Distinct Topics & SEO: If each section has a lot of unique content (e.g., "About Us," "Contact," "Blog"), separate pages are better for organization and Search Engine Optimization (SEO), as search engines index individual URLs.
  • Bookmarking and Sharing: Users can easily bookmark or share a link to a specific page.
  • Content Scalability: If a section is expected to grow significantly (like a news feed), a dedicated page is more scalable than a tab.
